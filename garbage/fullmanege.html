Garbage & Waste Management

Understanding proper waste disposal for a cleaner environment

What is Garbage?

Garbage refers to waste materials that are discarded as no longer useful or required. It includes various types of waste generated from different sources in our daily lives.

Types of Garbage

  • Organic Waste: Biodegradable materials like food scraps, yard trimmings
  • Recyclables: Paper, cardboard, glass, metals, certain plastics
  • Hazardous Waste: Batteries, chemicals, electronics (need special disposal)
  • Residual Waste: Non-recyclable, non-hazardous materials

Proper Garbage Disposal Methods

The Waste Hierarchy

  1. Reduce: Minimize waste generation
  2. Reuse: Find new uses for items
  3. Recycle: Process materials into new products
  4. Recover: Energy from waste (incineration)
  5. Dispose: Landfill as last option

Common Disposal Techniques

  • Recycling: Separating recyclable materials for processing
  • Composting: Breaking down organic waste into nutrient-rich soil
  • Landfills: Engineered sites for safe waste containment
  • Incineration: Burning waste with energy recovery where possible

Why Proper Garbage Management Matters

  • Reduces environmental pollution
  • Conserves natural resources through recycling
  • Prevents spread of diseases
  • Reduces greenhouse gas emissions
  • Saves landfill space

Common Garbage-Related Terms

  • Municipal Solid Waste (MSW): Everyday items discarded by the public
  • Waste Stream: The flow of waste from source to disposal
  • Source Reduction: Reducing waste at its origin
  • Upcycling: Creating higher-value products from waste materials

Reduce Your Garbage Footprint

5 Simple Steps

  1. Follow the 3 R's: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
  2. Choose products with minimal packaging
  3. Compost food and yard waste
  4. Donate usable items instead of throwing them away
  5. Use reusable containers and bags
